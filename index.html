Collected Poems of Tagore - 泰戈尔诗选

Posted on 2020-05-05 | In Poetry | Visitors:
Words count in article:

译者: Zhengjie

Link: Collected Poems of Tagore - 泰戈尔诗选

小记： 这篇整理出来的是泰戈尔《飞鸟集》和《新月集》中作者比较喜欢的一些诗。作者曾在高中的时候不经意间读了一些句子，觉得泰戈尔的诗朴实而脱俗，甚是喜欢。于是将这两本诗作为英语学习材料学习，进行摘录和背诵。大学放假在家，整理杂物时看到了这本整理手记，就想放到博客上，一方面重读有不一样的感悟，一方面博客也方便写一些属于自己的理解，回头再看。泰格尔作品中的哲学，就像很多东方哲学、中国哲学，并不着重于思辨，而是关注于经验。泰戈尔的作品是一个巨大的宝藏，能启迪人们思考各种意义上的“意义”。有意思的是，每次读泰戈尔的诗，脑子里浮现的却是哈利波特中邓布利多教授的神态和声音，或许他们俩有很多相似之处吧。读他的文字让人感到平静，我想这也是泰戈尔所希望带给读者的。用他自己的一句诗来评价：“Your voice, my friend, wanders in my heart, like the muffled sound of the sea among these listening pines.” 不知道在这个时代以及以后的日子里，还有没有人能写出这样的诗。由于是gedit纯手打，可能会有纰漏。有些地方写了注释。供参考。

Poems from Stray Birds

  1. The world puts off its mask of vastness to its lover. It becomes small as one song, as one kiss of the eternal.

  2. If you shed tears when you miss the sun, you also miss the stars.

  3. Sorrow is hushed into peace in my heart like the evening among the silent trees.

  4. Some unseen fingers, like an idle breeze, are playing upon my heart the music of ripples.

  5. My wishes are fools, they shout across the song, Master. Let me but listen.

  6. I cannot choose the best. The best chooses me.

  7. That I exist is a perpetual surprise which is life.

  8. Life finds its wealth by the claims of the world, and its worth by the claims of love.

  9. I cannot tell why this heart languishes in silence. It is for small needs it never asks, or knows or remembers.

  10. You smiled and talked to me of nothing and I felt that for this I have been waiting long.

  11. The world rushes on over the strings of the lingering heart making the music of sadness.

  12. God finds himself by creating.

  13. The stars are not afraid to appear like fireflies.

  14. The mind, sharp but not broad, stick at every point but does not move.

  15. Man does not reveal himself in his history, he struggles up through it.

  16. Like the meeting of the seagulls and the waves we meet and come near. The seagulls fly off, the waves roll away and we depart.

  17. Life is given to us, we earn it by giving it.

  18. We come nearest to great when we are great in humility.

  19. Never be afraid of the moments —— this sings the voice of the everlasting.

  20. The perfect decks itself in beauty for the love of the imperfect.

  21. God says to man, “I heal you therefore I hurt, love you therefore punish.”

  22. The infant flower opens its bud and cries, “Dear world, please do not fade.”

  23. God grows weary of great kingdoms, but never of little flowers.

  24. Wrong cannot afford defeat but Right can.

  25. “I give my whole water in joy, “ sing the waterfall, “though little of it is enough for the thirsty.”

  26. The woodcutter’s axe begged for its handle from the tree. The tree gave it.

  27. The mist, like love, plays upon the heart of the hills and bring out surprises of beauty.

  28. We read the world wrong and say that it deceives us.

  29. Every child comes with the message that God is not yet discouraged of man.

  30. Your voice, my friend, wanders in my heart, like the muffled sound of the sea among these listening pines.

  31. Man barricades against himself.

  32. The grass seeds her crowd in the earth. The tree seeks his solitude of the sky.

  33. What is this unseen flame of darkness whose sparks are the stars.

  34. Let life be beautiful like summer flowers and death like autumn leaves.

  35. He who wants to do good knocks at the gate; he who loves find the gate open.

  36. In death the many becomes one; in life the one become many. Religion will be one when God is dead.

  37. The artist is the lover of Nature, therefore he is her slave and her master.

  38. “How far are you from me, O fruit?” “I am hidden in your heart, O flower.”

  39. “You are the big drop of dew under the lotus leaf, I am the smaller one on its upper side, “ said the dewdrop to the lake.

  40. The scabbard is content to be dull when it protects the keenness of the sword.

  41. Power said to the world, “You are mine.” The world kept it prisoner on her throne. Love said to the world, “I am thine.” The world gave it freedom to her house.

  42. Be still, my heart, these great trees are prayers.

  43. The noise of the moment scoffs at the music of the Eternel.

  44. Death’s stamp gives value to the coin of life; making it possible to buy with life what is truly precious.

  45. The cloud stood humbly in a corner of the sky. The morning crowned it with splendour.

  46. The dust receives insult and in return offers her flowers.

  47. Do not linger to gather flowers to keep them, but walk on, for flowers will keep themselves blooming all your way.

  48. The music of the far-away summer flutters around the autumn seeking its former nest.

  49. Do not insult your friend by lending him merits from your own pocket.

  50. Man goes into the noisy crowd to drown his own clamour of silence.

  51. The sun has his simple rode of light. The clouds are decked with gorgeousness.

  52. The hills are like shouts of children who raise their arms, trying to catch stars.

  53. Dear friend, I feel the silence of your great thoughts of many a deepening eventide on this beach when I listen to these waves.

  54. The bird thinks it is an act of kindness to give the fish a life in the air.

  55. “In the moon thou sendest thy love letters to me, “ said the night to the sun, “I leave my answers in tears upon the grass.”

  56. Not hammer-strokes, but dance of the water sings the pebbles into perfection.

  57. To be outspoken is easy when you do not wait to speak the complete truth.

  58. If you shut your door to all errors, truth will be shut out.

  59. Leisure in its activity is work. The stillness of the sea stirs in waves.

  60. The leaf becomes flower when it loves. The flower become fruit when it worships.

  61. When I travelled to here and there, I was tired of thee, O Road, but now when thou leadest me to everywhere I am wedded to thee in love.

  62. Let me think that there is one among those stars that guides my life through the dark unknown.

  63. I have my stars in the sky. But, oh, for my little lamp unlit in my house.

Poems from The Crescent Moon
