译者: Zhengjie

Link: Collected Poems of Tagore - 泰戈尔诗选

小记： 这篇整理出来的是泰戈尔《飞鸟集》和《新月集》中作者比较喜欢的一些诗。作者曾在高中的时候不经意间读了一些句子，觉得泰戈尔的诗朴实而脱俗，甚是喜欢。于是将这两本诗作为英语学习材料学习，进行摘录和背诵。大学放假在家，整理杂物时看到了这本整理手记，就想放到博客上，一方面重读有不一样的感悟，一方面博客也方便写一些属于自己的理解，回头再看。泰格尔作品中的哲学，就像很多东方哲学、中国哲学，并不着重于思辨，而是关注于经验。泰戈尔的作品是一个巨大的宝藏，能启迪人们思考各种意义上的“意义”。有意思的是，每次读泰戈尔的诗，脑子里浮现的却是哈利波特中邓布利多教授的神态和声音，或许他们俩有很多相似之处吧。读他的文字让人感到平静，我想这也是泰戈尔所希望带给读者的。用他自己的一句诗来评价：“Your voice, my friend, wanders in my heart, like the muffled sound of the sea among these listening pines.” 其中，也不乏深刻而惊艳的句子。由于是gedit纯手打，可能会有纰漏。有些地方写了注释。供参考。

Poems from The Crescent Moon

The First Jasmines
Ah, these jasmines, these white jasmines!
I seem to remember the first day when I filled my hands with these jasmines, these white jasmines.
I have loved the sunlight, the sky and the green earth;
I have heard teh liquid murmur of the river through the darkness of midnight;
Autumn sunsets have come to me at the bend of a road in the lonely waste, like a bride raising her veil to accept her lover.
Yet my memory is still sweet with the first white jasmines that I held in my hand when I was a child.
Many a glad day has come in my life, and I have laughed with merry makers on festival nights.
On grey mornings of rain I have crooned many an idle song.
I have worn round my neck the evening wreath of bakulas woven by the hand of love.
Yet my heart is sweet with the memory of the first fresh jasmines that filled my hands when I was a child.

The Land of the Exile
The shadow of the rains has covered the days from end to end. The fierce lightning is scratching the sky with its nails.

Authorship
You say that father writes a lot of books, but what he writes I don’t understand.
He was reading to you all the evening, but could you really make out what he meant?
What nice stories, mother, you can tell us! Why can’t father write like that, I wonder?
Did he never hear from his own mother stories of giants and fairies and princesses?
Has he forgotten them all?
Often when he gets late for his bath you have to go and call him an hundred times.
You wait and keep his dishes warm for him, but he goes on writting and forgets.
Father always plays at making books.
If ever I go to play in father’s room, you come and call me, “What a naughty child!”
If I make the slightest noise, you say, “Don’t you see that father’s at his work?”
What’s the fun of always writing and writing?
When I take up father’s pen or pencil and write upon his book just as he does —— a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, i, —— why do you get across with me, then, mother?
You never say a word when father writes.
When my father wastes such heaps of paper, mother, you don’t seem to mind at all.
But if I take only one sheet to make a boat with, you say, “Child, how troublesome you are!”
What do you think of father’s spoiling sheets and sheets of paper with black marks all ovr on both sides?
